Eligibility Criteria for Unsecured OD Limit

  • Eligibility criteria for a dropline overdraft loan may differ among financial institutions, but certain entities can generally avail of this facility if they meet the criteria set by the institution. Common entities eligible for this facility include private limited companies, sole proprietors, self-employed professionals, entrepreneurs, and partnership firms.

It's crucial to note that entities beyond those mentioned above may also qualify for this facility, and individuals can check their eligibility by visiting the financial institution's website.

Typically, eligibility criteria set by financial institutions revolve around factors such as age at the time of availing the facility and at the end of the tenure, turnover and profitability of the business, business vintage, and proof of office/residence. However, these criteria may vary between institutions, emphasising the importance of checking eligibility before applying. This ensures a seamless application process and enhances the likelihood of approval.

To avail of the benefits of overdraft facilities offered by banks and financial institutions, individuals must meet specific eligibility criteria, including:

  • Age Criteria: Applicants must be at least 21 years old.
  • Bank Account Requirements: A requirement to have an account with the bank offering the overdraft facility.
  • Income Criteria: Varies among lenders, with a preference for applicants employed in reputed organisations.
  • Good CIBIL or Credit Score: While not a crucial factor, a good CIBIL score is considered advantageous.
  • Business Vintage: Varies between banks, but generally, institutions prefer self-employed applicants with a proven track record of running a profitable business for several years.
